About Georgia

Georgia is a developing country located at the intersection of East Europe and West Asia. It shares its borders with Russia to the East, Turkey and Armenia to the South, bordered by the Black Sea to the North. With a land area of 69,700 sq. km. and a population of approximately 4 million it maintains good relations with India.

FAQs

1. Is it a safe place for Indian students to pursue an MBBS course in Georgia?

Yes, Georgia is considered a safe country with low crime rates. It is a top choice for many Indian students due to its quality education and safe environment.

2. Is it mandatory for Indian students to qualify for the NEET exam before MBBS admission in Georgia?

Indian students must qualify for the NEET UG exam as mandated by the Indian government before seeking admission to Georgian universities.

3. Is FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Exam) compulsory to practice in India after completing medical studies in Georgia?

FMGE is a mandatory exam conducted by the National Medical Commission for obtaining a practicing license in India.

4. What is the cost per year to study MBBS in MCI/NMC-approved medical colleges in Georgia?

The tuition fees for international students in Georgia's medical colleges range from USD 4000 to USD 7000 annually, varying depending on the college and ownership.

5. Is Georgia a secure place for Indian medical students?

Yes, Georgia and India have had positive relations since 1955, and India recognizes Georgia as a country. As a result, the safety of Indian medical students is ensured, and the government provides a welcoming environment for them.
